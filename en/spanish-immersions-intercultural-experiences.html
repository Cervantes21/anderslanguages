Experience the culture

TEXT OK


During the (optional) Intercultural Experiences, you will explore the culture behind the Spanish language through its history, gastronomy, and festivals...

Taste of History

Your instructors will be your guides during all excursions. They will provide opportunities to practice your Spanish in various contexts and offer insights into the history and culture of the Spanish-speaking world..

Taste of History

MEXICO

Mexico, as we know it today, was founded in 1810, but human settlements date back to 2000 BC. Join your Spanish instructors as we take you back to pre-Hispanic and colonial times...


Offered all year round

Taste of History

SPAIN

The history of southern Spain has been strongly influenced by three major religions: Judaism, Islam, and Christianity. As we visit important landmarks and cities, you'll discover how these three religions shaped Spain.


Offered all year round

Gastronomic

Your Spanish instructors will take you to the most authentic places to eat, often overlooked by tourists. You'll learn about the local ingredients at the colorful markets and prepare a meal with our chef.

Gastronomic

MEXICO

In 2010, Mexican cuisine became part of a small group of countries and regions whose food is recognized by UNESCO as an Intangible Cultural Heritage. Mexico's cultural and natural diversity is best shown through its food. With your Spanish instructors, you will visit the colorful markets. You will learn about the ingredients and try them in the traditional food stalls, where women prepare simple but delicious dishes.


Offered all year round

Gastronomic

SPAIN

Spain has got some of the best food in the world. Paella, Spanish omelet, and Iberian ham are world-famous. But there's more to it than that. The country has got hundreds of miles of rivers and is surrounded by the Cantabrian Sea, the Mediterranean Sea, and the Atlantic. All that water means lots of great seafood. And of course, Spain also has some of the best wines in the world.


Offered all year round

DEBBIE B

Definitely, Spain is so much more than paella, Iberian ham, olives and wine. I had a great time eating fresh fish in a chiringuito on the beach surrounded by the dunes of La Doñana national Park. Or having a meal at the local foodmarket, where everything is prepared with fresh ingredients straight from the counter. And of course, preparing an arroz negro during a cooking class. This was a great way to improve my Spanish! And good to know: although you can eat tortillas in Spain and Mexico, they are different things :-)
